# AI Spend Audit - User Research Notes

## Overview
These insights are based on informal conversations with developers and students exploring AI tools for daily use. The goal was to understand how users think about AI spending, cost tracking, and tool selection.

---

## 1. Informal Conversation - Student Developer

**Profile:** 3rd year Computer Science student

**Key feedback:**
- “I just pay for ChatGPT Plus, I never really calculate if it’s worth it monthly.”
- “Most of my friends use AI tools but no one tracks spending properly.”

**Insight:**
Users in learning stage do not actively monitor AI expenses unless costs become noticeable.

**Impact on design:**
Simplified input form and avoided complex finance-style dashboards.

---

## 2. Informal Conversation - Freelance Developer

**Profile:** Freelance web developer using OpenAI API

**Key feedback:**
- “API billing is confusing, I just check the bill at the end of the month.”
- “Sometimes I don’t even know which project used how much tokens.”

**Insight:**
There is a lack of clarity between usage and cost breakdown.

**Impact on design:**
Added clear per-tool cost segmentation in audit results.

---

## 3. Informal Conversation - Early Stage Startup Team Member

**Profile:** Developer at a small startup (3–5 members)

**Key feedback:**
- “We use multiple AI tools but never optimized subscriptions.”
- “We probably waste money but don’t have time to analyze it.”

**Insight:**
Small teams prioritize speed over cost optimization.

**Impact on design:**
Focused on instant audit results instead of detailed onboarding.

---

## Summary Insight
Across conversations, a consistent pattern emerged: users value speed and simplicity over detailed financial tracking. Most are aware they may be overspending but lack tools that make optimization easy and fast.
